Margarita Recipe

Description

The Margarita, a timeless classic, offers a tantalizing blend of tangy and refreshing flavors.
This iconic cocktail features smooth tequila, zesty lime juice, and the subtle sweetness of triple sec.
Served over ice, its invigorating citrus aroma lures you in.
With a perfect balance of sour and sweet, the Margarita dances on your taste buds, leaving a delightful finish that beckons for another sip.

Whether it's a summer fiesta or a cozy night in, this versatile cocktail promises to transport you to a sun-kissed beach with every intoxicating sip.
Cheers to the enchanting Margarita, a fiesta in a glass!

Ingredients

  • Tequila - 60 ml
  • Triple sec or Cointreau (orange liqueur) - 30 ml
  • Freshly squeezed lime juice - 30 ml
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional:Lime wedges or wheels for garnish
  • Optional:Salt for rimming the glass

Steps

  1. Rim the glass (optional):
    • If desired,rim the edge of a cocktail glass with salt.
      To do this, take a lime wedge and run it along the outer edge of the glass to moisten it.
      Then, dip the glass into a plate of salt or sugar, rotating it until the rim is coated.
  2. Add ingredients:
    • Fill a cocktail shaker with ice cubes
    • Pour in the tequila, triple sec or Cointreau, and freshly squeezed lime juice
  3. Shake:
    • Securely close the cocktail shaker and shake it vigorously fo about 10-15 seconds
      This chills the drink and mixes the flavours.
  4. Strain and Serve:
    • If you rimmed the glass, strain the cocktail into the preparated glass filled with ice.
    • If you skipped rimming the glass, strain the cocktail into a regular cocktail into a regular cocktail glass filled with ice.
  5. Garnish (optional):
    • Add a lime wedge or wheel to the rim of the glass for an extra touch of presentation.
  6. Enjoy:
    • Your classic Margarita is now ready to be savored. Sip and enjoy responsibly!

Remember, there are various ways to customize a Margarita to your liking. You can ajust the
tequila-to-lime ratio or even add a splash of sweetener like simple syrup or agave nectar if you
prefer a sweeter taste. Cheers!
